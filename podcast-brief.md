# PERFORMANCE Podcast — Brief & One-Pager

> **Tagline:** *Le Podcast des Décideurs qui Courent*
> **Host:** Badr Zinoun | CTO-Partner @ Matem Africa & Valkima | Runner & Triathlete
> **Language:** Français × Darija × English

---

## 1. Concept & Vision

**PERFORMANCE** exists at the intersection of two worlds that rarely collide in Morocco: high-level business decision-making and elite athletic pursuit. This isn't a podcast about "work-life balance" (we hate that phrase). It's about how the discipline, resilience, and clarity you build through sport make you a better leader, founder, or executive.

**Vision:** Become the reference podcast for Moroccan decision-makers who train — the place where a CEO talks openly about their marathon split times, where a VC discusses their FTP on the bike, where ambition and endurance share the same microphone.

**The vibe:** Two amis qui discutent après une course — not a formal interview show, not a TED talk. Conversations between peers who happen to be crushing it in the boardroom *and* on the track. Real talk, real numbers, real struggle.

## 2. Positioning — What Makes It Unique

**No equivalent in the Moroccan market.** Here's why:

| Other Business Podcasts (MA) | PERFORMANCE |
|---|---|
| Corporate panel talk | Intimate 1-on-1 conversations |
| Suit-and-tie energy | Post-run energy |
| "Tell us about your success" | "What broke you and how did you fix it?" |
| Guests who *talk about* performance | Guests who *live* performance |
| French only | French + darija + English (code-switching is authentic) |

**Differentiator:** The guests are not chosen for their job title. They're chosen because they train. The sport angle is *structural*, not cosmetic. Every guest has a relationship with discipline through physical pursuit — running, cycling, swimming, triathlon, trail, CrossFit, whatever. The business conversation flows *from* that foundation.

**Positioning statement:**
> PERFORMANCE is to Moroccan business what the morning miles are to its listeners: a daily practice that separates those who *talk* about elite performance from those who *do* it.

## 5. Content Pillars

Each episode touches at least two pillars. Every episode must have sport as the entry point, business as the substance.

### Pillar 1 — La Discipline Corporelle
How does training discipline translate to business decisions? Time management, consistency under pressure, the ability to suffer (and enjoy it). What does a CEO's training week actually look like?

*Sample episode theme: "Le CEO qui se lève à 5h pour Courir"*

### Pillar 2 — Échec & Résilience
The race that went wrong. The deal that collapsed. The DNS (Did Not Start) that taught more than any finish. Performance people have rich failure vocabularies — we go there.

*Sample episode theme: "J'ai DNF un ultra parce que j'ai Mal lu le Parcours"*

### Pillar 3 — Leadership par le Sport
Team captains, club founders, Strava club leaders. How does leading a pack in a race translate to leading a team in a merger? What sport teaches you about influence, trust, and pacing others.

*Sample episode theme: "Comment j'ai Coaché mon Équipe comme un Club de Course"*

### Pillar 4 — Le Corps comme Outil
Sleep, nutrition, injury prevention, heart rate zones, periodization of training. The decision-maker's body is their instrument. We treat it seriously — not as a flex, but as infrastructure.

*Sample episode theme: "J'ai Construit mon Corps comme mon Startup"*

## 6. Episode Format & Structure

### Standard Episode: 45–65 minutes

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **Intro / Hook** | 2–3 min | Host opens alone, sets the episode's energy, hook for today's topic |
| **Warm-up** | 5–8 min | Ice-breaker sport question — easy, human, disarming |
| **Bloc Principal** | 30–40 min | Core conversation, 2–3 content pillars explored in depth |
| **Cool-down** | 5–8 min | Quick-fire questions, lighter tone, future goals |
| **Outro / CTA** | 2–3 min | Host closes, subscribe prompt, social call-to-action |

### Flow Rules
- **No rigid script.** The structure is a guide, not a cage. Organic flow > mechanical following.
- **Sport first.** The episode must open through a sport lens before pivoting to business.
- **Honesty over polish.** If a guest says something real, let it breathe. Don't cut for time in the important moments.
- **Code-switching is welcome.** French with a darija expression when it lands better. English for technical sport terms (PR, DNS, FTP, RPE).

## 7. Host Voice & Style Guide

### Who Badr Is On Air
- **Tone:** Competent ami. Not the expert. The curious peer who asks the questions the listener would ask.
- **Energy:** Morning-run optimism meets after-work-beer honesty. Warm but never sycophantic.
- **Language:** French is default. Darija enters naturally for emphasis, humor, or authentic expression. English for sport terms and when a guest code-switches (roll with it).
- **Signature moves:**
  - The "wait, explain that like I'm a beginner" move
  - The "what was your lowest point through all this" follow-up
  - The direct transition: "Okay, on the course, you DNF'd — how did that mess with your head?"

### What Badr Doesn't Do
- ❌ Talk over guests to show he knows things
- ❌ Ask questions he already knows the answer to
- ❌ Use corporate buzzwords ("synergy," "disruption," "leverage")
- ❌ Rush the emotional moments
- ❌ Make it about him

### Style Cheat Sheet
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---|---|
| "That's very interesting" | "Wait, tell me more about that" |
| "Can you tell our audience about..." | "For the people who don't know, explain it" |
| "What are your keys to success?" | "What's the thing you almost got wrong?" |
| "In conclusion..." | Just... don't do conclusions in the middle |

```

```

5. **[Number] Things I Learned from [Guest Name]** — "12 choses que j'ai apprises de Fatima"
6. **[Nom] × [Guest Sport]: [Theme]** — "Yassir × Trail Running: Le mental, c'est tout"
7. **[Guest Name] au start de [event]** — "Nabil au start du 20 km de Rabat"
8. **[Nom]: Le CEO qui [sport action]** — "Karim: Le CEO qui Court Sans Montre"

10. **[Nom] s'est planté à [event], et voici pourquoi c'était une lecon** — "Hicham s'est planté au 10K, et voici pourquoi c'était une leçon"
11. **[Guest Name]: Quand le business copy la course** — "Badr: Quand le business copy la course"
12. **[Nom] pendant [time period]** — "Amine pendant les 12 semaines avant le marathon"
13. **[Guest Name] n'a pas terminé [event]** — "Youssef n'a pas terminé l'ultra-trail, et ça l'a rendu meilleur"
14. **[Guest Name] du boardroom au [sport]** — "Loubna du boardroom au crossfit"
15. **[Guest Name], [company] × [sport discipline]** — "Mehdi, OCP × Course à pied"
16. **[Guest Name] + [Guest Name]: [Event name] Special** — "Oussama + Hamza: Marathon Special"
17. **[Guest Sport] avec [Guest Name]** — "VTT avec Rachid, le CFO qui grimpe"
18. **[Guest Name]: [Number] mois avant [event]** — "Sara: 6 mois avant le Ironman"
19. **[Nom] parle [sport discipline] comme [metaphor]** — "Fouad parle course comme une startup"
20. **[Guest Name] a échoué, a réessayé, a réussi [event]** — "Hicham a échoué, a réessayé, a finish le 20K"

### Naming Rules
- Keep it punchy, max 60 characters if possible
- Include the guest name for personal connection
- Reference a specific sport element (km, race, discipline, number) immediately
- No empty adjectives ("incredible," "exclusive" — zero interest)

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|

| Shownotes published | Same day as episode |
